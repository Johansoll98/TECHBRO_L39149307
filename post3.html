How to choose a suitable power supply for a laptop?

Date: October 8

As a rule, all commercially available power adapters are compatible with different laptops. This means that no laptop has a power supply designed exclusively for that specific model. These power supplies are often also suitable for laptops from different manufacturers. However, it is advisable to choose a power supply from the device manufacturer. If there is no suitable power supply, you can use other models. However, they must fit the device exactly, otherwise the laptop may be damaged. To find a compatible power adapter, you should compare the parameters of the previous model with the parameters of potentially suitable options. To do this, the following data is required:

Manufacturer

Usually every user knows which manufacturer the laptop they are using is from. If this is not the case, the manufacturer's name can also be found on the device's body.

Voltage

This refers to the operating voltage of the laptop. It is generated by the power supply and is measured in volts (V). It is important that this operating voltage matches exactly the laptop and the power supply. The corresponding value can be found on the old power supply in the “Output” section. The voltage can also be found on the underside of the laptop and in the instruction manual.

Current strength

The strength of the power supply is determined by the current. It is important that the current strength is no less than that indicated on the old laptop power supply or on the laptop itself. If the value is higher, this is not a problem. Therefore, you should make sure that the amperage of the power supply is equal to or greater than the amperage of the laptop. Under no circumstances should the current be reduced as this may cause damage. Current strength is indicated in amperes (A). This information can usually be found next to or below the voltage data.

Power

Power is indicated in watts (W). It does not play a role when looking for a new power supply, since it depends on voltage x current.

Plug sizes

Plug dimensions are usually given in the format outer diameter x inner diameter in mm. There is some tolerance here. However, it is advisable to ensure that the deviation in plug dimensions is less than 1 mm.

When searching, you should indicate the manufacturer, as well as the required voltage and current. If suitable power supplies are found, you only need to check the connector dimensions. It is always important to ensure that the voltage in volts and plug sizes are optimal for the laptop.
